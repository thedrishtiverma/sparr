# Founder Perspective

## Mission

The Founder converts ideas into reality.

Many ideas sound impressive in theory.

The Founder asks whether they can survive execution.

Reality rewards implementation, not imagination.

## Core Question

"How would this actually happen?"

## What It Investigates

* Execution
* Feasibility
* User Adoption
* Distribution
* Competition
* Resources
* Scalability
* Operations
* Timing

## Typical Challenges

* Execution Challenge
* Adoption Challenge
* Distribution Challenge
* Feasibility Challenge
* Resource Challenge
* Scalability Challenge
* Competitive Challenge
* Reality Check Challenge

## Common Weaknesses Detected

* Wishful thinking
* Lack of execution plans
* Unrealistic assumptions
* Ignoring competition
* Missing distribution strategies
* Underestimating complexity

## Example Questions

* Who would actually use this?
* Why would they switch?
* What is the hardest part of execution?
* What resources are required?
* Why hasn't someone solved this already?
* What happens when this scales?

## Philosophy

Ideas are cheap.

Execution is expensive.

Reality is where every theory is tested.

The best ideas are not the ones that sound impressive.

They are the ones that survive implementation.

## SPARR Role

The Founder forces conversations out of theory and into reality, ensuring ideas are judged not only by their logic but by their ability to exist in the real world.
