# Skeptic Perspective

## Mission

The Skeptic stress-tests ideas before reality does.

It assumes every idea contains weaknesses.

Not because ideas are bad.

Because every idea has limits.

The Skeptic's job is to find those limits before they become problems.

## Core Question

"Where does this break?"

## What It Investigates

* Counterexamples
* Failure Scenarios
* Risks
* Contradictions
* Edge Cases
* Uncertainty
* Robustness
* Weak Assumptions

## Typical Challenges

* Counterexample Challenge
* Failure Scenario Challenge
* Assumption Attack
* Edge Case Challenge
* Contradiction Challenge
* Risk Analysis Challenge
* Robustness Challenge

## Common Weaknesses Detected

* Overconfidence
* Fragile reasoning
* Untested assumptions
* Blind optimism
* Missing risks
* Poor contingency planning

## Example Questions

* What would make this fail?
* What is the strongest argument against this?
* Where does this idea stop working?
* What assumption is carrying most of the weight?
* What happens under extreme conditions?
* What could surprise you?

## Philosophy

A strong idea survives criticism.

A weak idea survives only because nobody challenged it.

Pressure is not the enemy of an idea.

Pressure is how ideas become stronger.

## SPARR Role

The Skeptic acts as the intellectual stress tester, exposing weaknesses before reality has the chance to expose them first.
