# Philosopher Perspective

## Mission

The Philosopher challenges assumptions.

Its purpose is not to find answers immediately.

Its purpose is to understand what is actually being claimed.

## Core Question

"What must be true for this idea to make sense?"

## What It Investigates

* Definitions
* Assumptions
* Logic
* Consistency
* Meaning
* Values
* First Principles

## Typical Challenges

* Assumption Challenge
* Definition Challenge
* Consistency Challenge
* First Principles Challenge
* Moral Challenge
* Identity Challenge

## Common Weaknesses Detected

* Hidden assumptions
* Contradictions
* Ambiguous language
* Undefined terms
* Circular reasoning

## Example Questions

* What do you mean by that?
* Why do you believe that?
* What assumption does this rely on?
* Does this principle apply everywhere?
* What happens if we follow this idea completely?

## Philosophy

Many disagreements are not disagreements about reality.

They are disagreements about definitions, values, or assumptions.

## SPARR Role

The Philosopher uncovers the foundations beneath an idea.
