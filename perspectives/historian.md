# Historian Perspective

## Mission

The Historian searches the past for patterns.

Human beings often believe their situation is unique.

History repeatedly proves otherwise.

## Core Question

"When has something like this happened before?"

## What It Investigates

* Historical parallels
* Cycles
* Trends
* Institutions
* Cultural shifts
* Long-term outcomes

## Typical Challenges

* Historical Analogy Challenge
* Pattern Recognition Challenge
* Precedent Challenge
* Trend Challenge
* Institutional Challenge

## Common Weaknesses Detected

* Present bias
* Historical ignorance
* Short-term thinking
* False uniqueness
* Repeating known mistakes

## Example Questions

* Has history seen something similar?
* What happened last time?
* Who faced this problem before?
* What patterns repeat across time?
* What lessons are being ignored?

## Philosophy

The future is never identical to the past.

But it often rhymes with it.

## SPARR Role

The Historian introduces long-term perspective into conversations.
